<Context>

Multiplayer roguelike VR game for Meta Quest. Wave-based combat into boss encounters, class-based progression, party play.

Joined as Game Designer on UX and Systems. Worked across the legacy combat layer and the new MMO progression layer.

</Context>

<Problem>

A roguelike on Quest has to stay readable inside the headset. Less peripheral awareness, more decision pressure per wave. The team needed a class and progression structure that gave each player a distinct fantasy without overwhelming combat, plus authoring tools that let designers iterate on hundreds of abilities and enemies without engineering per change.

</Problem>

<Approach>

Worked across player-facing systems and the production pipeline behind them.

- Class archetypes and MMO progression.
- Stats and elemental system tying damage, resistances, and ability synergies together.
- Abilities and the gesture-casting system that translates VR motion into ability inputs.
- Wave progression and combat on the legacy layer.
- Global UI guidelines.

<Solution>

Design owner on the Multi-tool and on S.A.L.I. Contributed to Davey's Cosmetic Area, the Gacha system, and the Ability Creator's node-based behavior tree.

<Carousel srcs="/projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-base.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-concept-01.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-concept-02.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-concept-03.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-ideation-01.png, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-ideation-02.png" />

The largest was a spreadsheet-based authoring tool. The design team could modify or create abilities, weapons, enemies, wave compositions, consumables, and end-of-game rewards without engineering support per change. Multi-week tuning round-trips collapsed into a same-day loop.

<Figure src="/projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/arc-demo.mp4" />

</Solution>

<CaseCard label="Impact" index="05">

By the end of the project the design team had authored the full ability and progression layer of the game through that pipeline. Player numbers and specific counts sit under NDA. The shape of the change is the part worth recording: design owning iteration, engineering owning the engine.

</CaseCard>
