<Context>

Highstreet: Calamity is a multiplayer roguelike VR game built for Meta Quest. Players fight through waves of enemies toward boss encounters, earning rewards and progressing alongside friends across a class-based system.

I joined as a Game Designer focused on UX and Systems, working across the legacy combat layer and the new MMO progression layer that anchors long-term play.

</Context>

<Problem>

A roguelike on Quest needs to feel readable inside a headset — players have less peripheral awareness than on a flat screen, and decision pressure compounds across waves. The team needed a class and progression structure that gave each player a distinct fantasy without overwhelming the moment-to-moment combat, plus authoring tools that let designers iterate on hundreds of abilities, enemies, and reward tables without engineering support per change.

</Problem>

<Approach>

I worked across multiple layers of the game to shape both player-facing systems and the production pipeline behind them:

- Class archetype and progression design for the MMO layer.
- Stats and the elemental system that ties damage, resistances, and ability synergies together.
- Abilities and the gesture-casting system that translates VR motion into ability inputs.
- Wave progression and combat systems on the legacy layer.
- Global UI guidelines and user experience patterns.

<Solution>

I was a major contributor and design owner on the Multi-tool and on S.A.L.I., and assisted on Davey's Cosmetic Area and the Gacha system, working with engineering on several data-driven systems along the way.

<Carousel srcs="/projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-base.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-concept-01.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-concept-02.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-concept-03.mp4, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-ideation-01.png, /projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/multitool-ideation-02.png" />

The biggest of those was a spreadsheet-based authoring tool that let the design team modify or create abilities, weapons, enemies, wave compositions, consumables, and end-of-game rewards without engineering support for each change. The tool collapsed a multi-week tuning round-trip into a same-day iteration loop.

I also contributed to the Ability Creator, which shaped the node-based behavior tree implementation underneath enemy AI and player abilities.

<Figure src="/projects/highstreet-calamity-vr/arc-demo.mp4" />

</Solution>

<CaseCard label="Impact" index="05">

The spreadsheet authoring tool changed how the design team worked day-to-day. The team moved from waiting on engineering for each tuning pass to authoring abilities, weapons, enemies, wave compositions, consumables, and reward tables directly — a multi-week round-trip collapsed into a same-day loop.

By the end of the project the design team had authored the full ability and progression layer of the game through that pipeline. Player numbers, specific counts, and the rest of the Impact section sit under NDA, but the shape of the change — design owning iteration, engineering owning the engine — is the part worth recording.

</CaseCard>
