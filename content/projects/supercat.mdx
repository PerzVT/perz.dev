<CaseCard label="Overview" index="01">

Supercat (previously Project Gigi) is a fast-paced action platformer where you play as a mecha cat tearing through challenging levels with a fluid, skill-expressive movement kit. The core fantasy is simple: the power of being limitless. Pounce across walls, slide into enemies, chain combat with parkour, and keep the flow intact as long as your mastery holds up.

<CaseCard label="My Role" index="02">

As Game Designer, I owned the design of the core mechanics and systems. I also contributed to level design whiteboxing and worked on shaders and technical art.

- Designed the core movement kit (wall-running, pouncing, slide-jumping, ramp-hopping) so that every input was combinable with every other input. Emergent skill over prescribed combos.
- Designed state-dependent weapon mechanics, where the same weapon behaves differently based on the player's movement state (sliding, airborne, grounded). This pushed players to learn the kit rather than lean on one dominant option.
- Designed the combat loop to incentivize pushing forward and engaging with new threats instead of backing off.
- Designed the reward loops to drive replayability and mastery-based engagement.
- Assisted with level design and whitebox/blockout, creating spaces that taught the movement kit naturally.
- Contributed to technical art and shaders to support the visual language of the game.
- Represented the project at public playtest booths at game developer summits in Edmonton and Calgary, gathering player feedback firsthand.
- Pitched the game at Indie Ignition Accelerator and other industry events.

</CaseCard>

<CaseCard label="Reflection" index="03">

Supercat is one of the most creatively exciting projects I have worked on, and also one of the most instructive on what it actually takes to move a game from prototype to pitch. Putting it in front of publishers, developers, and real players at public booths changed how I think about how a game gets read by people outside the team. How much the feel of movement communicates before a single word of explanation is spoken.

The movement kit we developed, the pitch experience, and the connections made through Indie Ignition all carried forward into the projects that came after.

</CaseCard>
